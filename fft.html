Step 1 · Arrive Prepared
What to tell your Health Care Provider
Tap a card to expand. Bring your phone or print this page — walk the provider through each section.
"I am a commercial airline pilot. I have suffered an acute occupational exposure to pyrolyzed aviation synthetic engine oils and bleed air contaminants in a confined space."
"I am a crew member in your clinic for screening and testing for an inhalation injury, organophosphate poisoning, or carbon monoxide poisoning following a toxic fume event — sometimes referred to as Aerotoxic Syndrome — in flight."
If the provider is unfamiliar: Request a referral to a toxicologist, neurologist, or occupational medicine physician. Do not leave without a specimen collection plan.

Not all providers are familiar with aviation fume events. Prioritize these specialties:

  • Occupational Medicine PhysicianBest first stop — trained in workplace chemical exposures, can order the full panel and document occupational injury
  • ToxicologistIf organophosphate or CO poisoning is suspected; interprets cholinesterase and CO-Hb levels
  • PulmonologistFor respiratory symptoms — orders spirometry, HRCT, FeNO, and bronchial provocation testing
  • Neurologist / NeuropsychologistFor cognitive symptoms, peripheral neuropathy, gait changes, or tremor after exposure
  • Emergency DepartmentFor acute symptoms — headache, confusion, chest pain, difficulty breathing. Go immediately.
  • AME (Aviation Medical Examiner)Must be notified if symptoms could affect fitness for duty. Coordinate with your union rep first.
Avoid urgent care only: Many urgent care clinics cannot run the specialized tests required. Get to an ED or occupational medicine clinic with lab access.
Time-critical: Many of these markers have short half-lives and must be collected within hours of exposure to be meaningful.
Priority — Request All of These
  • Cholinesterase (RBC) URGENTFor organophosphate (TCP) exposure. Red blood cell cholinesterase reflects acute nerve agent/OP poisoning.
  • Carboxyhemoglobin (CO-Hb) URGENTBlood marker of carbon monoxide exposure. >3% abnormal in non-smoker. Degrades rapidly — collect immediately.
  • Acetylcholinesterase (AChE) URGENTCritical nervous system enzyme. Rapidly broken down after organophosphate exposure. Analyze immediately.
  • VOC Human Biomonitoring (HBM) URGENTMeasures toxic compounds or metabolites in blood, urine, or exhaled air. Short half-life — must be collected shortly after exposure.
  • Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)Detects poisonings, hypoxia. Includes arterial CO-Hb measurement.
  • Butyl Cholinesterase (BChE)Primary bioscavenger — binds organophosphates before they reach the CNS. Influenced by TCPs.
  • Serum / Plasma Cholinesterase (PChE)Detects organophosphate poisoning and occupational chemical exposure. Influenced by TCPs.
  • Organophosphate Human Biomonitoring (OP-HBM)TCP, TXP, TBP, TPP, DBPP, TiBP, BHT, PIP and metabolites (DoCP, DmCP, DpCP, DBP, DPP)
  • Serum C-Reactive Protein (CRP)Inflammation marker — elevated after organophosphate exposure.
  • Neuropathy Target Esterase (NTE)Biomarker for organophosphate exposure via blood or lymphocyte assay.
  • Pulse Oximetry (SpO2)Continuous monitoring of blood oxygen saturation.
  • Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)Baseline organ function assessment.

Request these alongside the immediate panel. Not as time-critical but important for baseline and documentation.

  • Methemoglobin (MetHb)Dysfunctional hemoglobin — cannot carry oxygen. Healthy level <1.5–2%.
  • Complete Blood Count (CBC / FBC)Baseline blood cell counts.
  • Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)Electrolytes, kidney function, glucose.
  • Liver Function Tests (LFTs) / Liver TransaminasesHepatotoxicity screening.
  • Creatinine (Cr)Kidney function assessment.
  • Blood GlucoseBaseline metabolic marker.
  • Lactate (LDH)Tissue hypoxia and cell damage marker.
  • Electrolytes (U&E)Sodium, potassium, chloride, bicarbonate.
  • ESR (EDTA)Erythrocyte sedimentation rate — inflammatory marker.
  • Fibrinogen (Sodium Citrate)Clotting and inflammatory marker.
  • Creatine Kinase with IsoenzymesMuscle damage assessment.
  • Muscle EnzymesTroponin, CKMM, CKMB, Aldolase — cardiac and skeletal muscle injury markers.
Time-sensitive: Organophosphate metabolites and VOCs are excreted in urine within hours. Collect as soon as possible.

Request analysis for all of the following:

  • Tricresyl Phosphate (TCP)Primary contaminant in Aerotoxic events.
  • TCP MetabolitesDoCP, DmCP, DpCP — breakdown products of TCP isomers.
  • Triphenyl Phosphate (TPP) + metabolite DPPDiphenyl phosphate (DPP).
  • Tributyl Phosphate (TBP) + metabolite DBPDibutyl phosphate (DBP).
  • Trixylyl Phosphate (TXP)
  • Dibutyl Phenyl Phosphate (DBPP)
  • Triisobutyl Phosphate (TiBP)
  • 2,6-di-tert-butyl-p-cresol (BHT)
  • Isopropylated Phosphate (PIP 3:1 / TIPP)
  • Mixed esters of TCP
  • 2,5-HexanedionMetabolite of n-hexane — solvent exposure marker.
Imaging
  • Posteroanterior (PA) + Lateral Chest X-rayInitial lung and cardiac imaging.
  • High Resolution CT Chest (HRCT)Detailed lung structure — identifies inflammation, fibrosis, air trapping.
Lung Function
  • Peak Flow / SpirometryBaseline airflow measurement. Document test date and time.
  • Lung Diffusion Capacity (DLCO)Gas transfer across alveoli. Note test timing relative to exposure.
  • Expired Nitric Oxide (FeNO)Airway inflammation marker.
  • Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)Complete respiratory and metabolic assessment.
  • Exercise Testing with SpO2 / Blood GasStress-induced respiratory assessment.
  • Ergo SpirometrySpirometry under controlled exercise load.
  • Bronchial Provocation TestingReactive airway assessment.
Cardiac
  • ECG (12-lead)Cardiac rhythm and ischemia screening after CO/chemical exposure.

Request these if you are experiencing cognitive changes, numbness, tingling, gait disturbances, weakness, tremor, or vision changes after a fume event.

Imaging & Objective Tests
  • Complete Neurologic ExamGait, vestibular function, focal deficits, weakness. Must be documented objectively.
  • MRI Brain ScanStructural neurological changes.
  • PET / SPECTFunctional brain imaging — metabolism and perfusion.
  • Electromyography (EMG)Muscle and motor nerve function testing.
  • Magnetic Resonance Neurography (MRN)High-resolution peripheral nerve imaging.
  • Skin Biopsy / ENFIntraepidermal nerve fiber density — small fiber neuropathy assessment.
  • Autonomic Nervous System Tests / Tilt TableDysautonomia assessment.
  • Nerve Conduction Velocity (NCVs)Peripheral nerve conduction assessment.
  • Quantitative Sensory Testing (QST)Standardized sensory threshold measurement.
  • Brainstem Audio Evoked PotentialAuditory pathway integrity.
  • Color Desaturation TestingVisual/optic nerve function.
Neurocognitive Battery
  • Neuropsychological AssessmentFull battery — processing speed, attention, reaction time, memory, problem-solving, cognitive flexibility
  • CALCAPSimple and choice reaction time tests.
  • Symbol Digit Modalities TestPsychomotor speed and attention.
  • Coding Test (WAIS)Processing speed and working memory.

Tell your provider about any of the following, and note when they started relative to the event:

  • Metallic or unusual taste in mouth
  • Burning or irritation of eyes, throat, or nasal passages
  • Headache — location, severity, onset time
  • Tingling or numbness in face, hands, or limbs
  • Dizziness, lightheadedness, or vertigo
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Cognitive changes — confusion, memory gaps, difficulty concentrating
  • Vision changes or blurring
  • Chest tightness, difficulty breathing, or coughing
  • Muscle weakness, tremor, or gait changes
  • Any observations reported by other crew members
Document now: Write down your symptoms and timestamps before this appointment. Memory of acute symptoms fades within hours and is critical for medical and legal documentation.
Mandatory Filing
Required Reports
All four reports are required after a fume or air quality event. File each independently — they serve different purposes and go to different systems.

ASAP Report
ProSafeT
Within 24 hrs

Aviation Safety Action Program report. Submit via ProSafeT as soon as possible after the event — 24-hour window from the time of occurrence.
